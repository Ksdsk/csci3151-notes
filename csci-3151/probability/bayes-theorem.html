Definition
...

A theorem describing the probability of an event based on prior knowledge of conditions that might be related to the event. It is very up-to interpretation and background of the solver.

Evidence should update your prior beliefs.

Heart of Bayes' Theorem
...

The heart of Bayes' theorem is useful for understanding what the equation actually means intuitively:

  1. First, consider all possibilities within the closed world.
  2. Second, check for all possibilities fitting the evidence.
  3. Finally, compute the probability for the subject given the evidence.

When to use Bayes' Rule
...

You can use the Bayes' rule in varieties of situation which fits the below description:

  1. You have a hypothesis,
  2. You have observed some evidence, and
  3. You want to compute the probability of the hypothesis given the evidence.
