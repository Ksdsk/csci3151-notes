Definition
...

Nearest neighbours is a simple classification algorithm that checks for the nearest point in the Euclidean space.

It's a very simple algorithm where no training is required - all computation can happen at the test time.

If the data is noisy, however, there could be room for misclassification (and it's highly sensitive). Therefore, we could use k-nearest neighbours and pick the majority vote to administrate for potential misclassification.

Performs a lot better with a good similarity measure.

Rule of thumb for K
...

Choosing a small k:

  • Good at capturing fine-grained patterns.
  • May overfit - be sensitive to random idiosyncrasies in the data.

Choosing a large k:

  • More stable predictions by averaging over lots of examples.
  • May underfit - fail to capture important regularities.
    • For example: dataset that has a striped pattern - will fail to capture such regularities if is too big.
